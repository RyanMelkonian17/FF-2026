News That Moves The Board

Offseason impact report
Blockbuster · June 1

A.J. Brown traded to New England

Philadelphia sent Brown to the Patriots for a 2028 first-rounder and a 2027 fifth. He reunites with Mike Vrabel and upgrades from a run-heavy offense to Drake Maye, who led the NFL in completion rate and YPA. Up: Brown, Maye, DeVonta Smith (PHI WR1 role). Down: Romeo Doubs and the rest of the NE target tree, Hurts' pass volume.

Teardown · Miami

Dolphins hit the reset button

Tua Tagovailoa was cut (signed with Atlanta), Tyreek Hill was released, and Jaylen Waddle was traded to Denver. Malik Willis takes over under center. Up: Waddle with Nix, Achane's target monopoly, Malik Washington. Down: the entire Miami passing-game ecosystem.

Trades · Veteran movement

Stars in new uniforms

Mike Evans joins the 49ers, Kenneth Walker III lands in Kansas City, Kyler Murray heads to Minnesota, Travis Etienne signs with New Orleans, Rico Dowdle joins Pittsburgh, and David Montgomery moves to Houston. Each move re-prices multiple players — see the boards above.

Regression watch · LA Rams

Stafford's age-38 masterpiece

Stafford led the NFL with 4,707 passing yards and a career-high 46 TDs at 38, and is now priced as a top-10 QB. Analysts are near-unanimous that the numbers dip. The Rams also drafted Alabama's Ty Simpson in Round 1 — the succession clock is running. Nacua's redraft value survives either way.

Coaching carousel

New play-callers, new values

Klint Kubiak takes over as Raiders head coach (bullish for Jeanty and Bowers), Drew Petzing becomes Lions OC (Gibbs remains featured), and Kevin Stefanski's arrival in Atlanta signals a heavy lead-back role for Bijan. Scheme changes are the cheapest edge in drafts.

Champs & runners-up

Seattle's title, New England's hangover?

Sam Darnold won the Super Bowl in a Seahawks offense that ranked 3rd in rush attempts and 29th in pass attempts — great for JSN's efficiency, poor for Darnold's fantasy volume. The Patriots, coming off a Super Bowl loss, responded by acquiring Brown. Don't buy the "Super Bowl loser curse" discount on Maye.

Models & Visuals

Where the value lives

Three views of the same truth: positional value is about the drop-off, not the raw points. Projections below are illustrative composites of expert consensus, indexed to full-PPR points per game.

The Value Cliff — projected PPR PPG by positional rank

How fast does each position fall off? TE craters after rank 2 (McBride/Bowers), RB holds a broad elite tier through ~8, WR declines gently (depth is real), and QB stays flat deep into the teens — which is why you can wait.

Scarcity Premium — PPG gap between rank 1 and rank 12

The bigger the bar, the more an elite starter at that position is worth over a replacement-level starter. This is the single best argument for paying up at TE and RB and waiting at QB.

Draft Capital Allocation — consensus positional mix, rounds 1–5 (12-team)

What the room will do before you do it. WRs dominate the first 60 picks; RBs cluster in rounds 1–2 and again in 4–5; the first QB run typically starts late round 3.

Draft-Day Playbook

How to use this board

1st & 10 — Rounds 1–3

Anchor with Bijan/Gibbs/CMC or an elite WR (Chase, Nacua, JSN). If the elite RBs are gone by your pick, pivot to WR without hesitation — the WR15 is much closer to the WR5 than the RB15 is to the RB5. Grab McBride or Bowers here only if they slide past their market price.

2nd & 6 — Rounds 4–8

This is where the 2026 draft is won. Target the discounted alphas (Lamb, Jefferson, A.J. Brown), the ascending sophomores (Jeanty, Hampton, Egbuka, McMillan), and one upside QB (Maye if he slides; otherwise Dart/Williams later). Avoid paying full price for Stafford's 2025.

4th & Goal — Rounds 9+

Swing exclusively for upside: Tuten, Gainwell, Tre Harris, Jadarian Price, Shough, Sadiq, the rookie WR trio. A late-round pick that returns a starter wins leagues; a late-round pick that returns a "safe" backup wins nothing. Draft two TEs only if you waited at the position.

Bye-week landmines

Weeks 6 and 11 are the heavy bye weeks this year — CIN/DET/MIA/MIN share Week 6, and ATL/NE/PHI/GB/SEA/LAR/CLE stack Week 11. Check your core's byes before you triple up on one week.
